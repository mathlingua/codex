Real Analysis

"Real Analysis"(#real.analysis)

" Analysis forms one of the major areas of mathematics. Whereas algebra deals with discrete structures, analysis deals with continuous structures. Informally, these are structures with elements that can be arbitrarily close by some definition of 'closeness'.

Real analysis focuses on the real number line that has a key characteristic that it 'does not contain any holes or gaps'. There are different ways to formalize what that means, but the approach that generalizes well to more abstract spaces is the concept of whether or not a Cauchy Sequence in a space necessarily converges.

In the real numbers, all Cauchy Sequences converge, and this is a defining characteristic that allows concepts such as limits, derivatives, integrals, series, and others to be defined. "
